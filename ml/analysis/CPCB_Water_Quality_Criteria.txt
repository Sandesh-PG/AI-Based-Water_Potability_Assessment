CPCB Water Quality Criteria - Designated Best Use Classification
Source: Central Pollution Control Board, India (IS 2296:1992)

CLASS A - Drinking Water Source without conventional treatment but after disinfection
- Total Coliforms: MPN/100ml shall be 50 or less
- pH: between 6.5 and 8.5
- Dissolved Oxygen (DO): 6 mg/l or more
- Biochemical Oxygen Demand (BOD) 5 days 20C: 2 mg/l or less

CLASS B - Outdoor Bathing (Organised)
- Total Coliforms: MPN/100ml shall be 500 or less
- pH: between 6.5 and 8.5
- Dissolved Oxygen (DO): 5 mg/l or more
- Biochemical Oxygen Demand (BOD) 5 days 20C: 3 mg/l or less

CLASS C - Drinking Water Source after conventional treatment and disinfection
- Total Coliforms: MPN/100ml shall be 5000 or less
- pH: between 6.0 and 9.0
- Dissolved Oxygen (DO): 4 mg/l or more
- Biochemical Oxygen Demand (BOD) 5 days 20C: 3 mg/l or less

CLASS D - Propagation of Wild life and Fisheries
- pH: between 6.5 and 8.5
- Dissolved Oxygen (DO): 4 mg/l or more
- Free Ammonia (as N): 1.2 mg/l or less

CLASS E - Irrigation, Industrial Cooling, Controlled Waste Disposal
- pH: between 6.0 and 8.5
- Electrical Conductivity at 25C: max 2250 micro mhos/cm
- Sodium Absorption Ratio (SAR): max 26
- Boron: max 2 mg/l

CLASS Below-E - Not Meeting A, B, C, D or E Criteria
- Water body does not meet any of the above designated use criteria
- Requires immediate remediation and pollution control action

PARAMETER VIOLATION THRESHOLDS SUMMARY (CPCB):
- BOD > 6 mg/l: Water body classified as polluted, action plan required
- DO < 4 mg/l: Critical oxygen depletion, harmful to aquatic life
- Total Coliforms > 5000 MPN/100ml: Unsafe for any drinking use
- Fecal Coliforms > 500 MPN/100ml: High health risk, indicates sewage contamination
- pH < 6.0 or > 9.0: Unsuitable for most uses

CAUSES OF PARAMETER VIOLATIONS:
- High BOD: Discharge of untreated sewage, industrial organic effluents, agricultural runoff
- Low DO: Excessive organic load, eutrophication, thermal pollution
- High Coliforms: Open defecation, untreated sewage discharge, poor sanitation infrastructure
- High Nitrates: Agricultural fertilizer runoff, sewage discharge, animal waste
- pH violations: Industrial chemical discharge, acid mine drainage, alkaline effluents

RECOMMENDED MEASURES:
- High BOD/Low DO: Establish sewage treatment plants (STPs), enforce industrial effluent standards
- High Coliforms: Improve sanitation infrastructure, enforce no-discharge zones, chlorination
- High Nitrates: Regulate fertilizer use, establish riparian buffer zones
- pH violations: Neutralization of industrial effluents before discharge, regular monitoring
